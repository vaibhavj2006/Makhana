From pond to pouch

Every seed starts life underwater, in a pond in Bihar.

Makhana comes from the seeds of the prickly water lily — grown in shallow wetland ponds across Bihar's Mithila region, which produces most of the world's supply.

The process

How a pond seed becomes a snack

01

Hand harvested

Farmers wade into the ponds to collect ripened seed pods by hand, a skill passed down through families in the region.

02

Sun dried

Harvested seeds are dried naturally over several days before the outer shell is removed.

03

Roasted & popped

Seeds are roasted in heated sand or on a tawa until they pop into the light, puffed shape you'd recognize.

04

Seasoned & packed

We add flavor in small batches and pack the same week — no long warehouse sit before it reaches you.

Why direct sourcing matters

Skipping the middlemen means better pay upstream, better quality downstream.

Most makhana sold globally passes through several layers of traders before reaching a brand. We buy directly from farming cooperatives in Bihar, which means farmers see more of the final price — and we get to trace every batch back to the pond it came from.

We visit our partner farms twice a year and publish harvest updates so you always know what season's batch you're eating.
